Alexander bridge

Alexander Bridge is located right between all of the best attractions in Paris and is considered as the most decorated bridge in Paris, this bridge is well worth a visit. The Alexander Bridge Paris, with its Art Nouveau lamps, winged horses statuses and nymphs connects the two banks of the Seine from the Champs-Elysees quarter to the Eiffel Tower quarter. The constructions, an impressive achievement for the time, started by the end of the 19th century to honor Tsar Alexander III and lasted several years until thje Universal Exposition of 1900. Alexander Bridge, Paris is a nice visit if you are coming from the Concorde square or Champs Elyse to the Eiffel tower or the Hotel des Invalides. Notice the similarities between the bridge and the Grand Palace behind it on the right bank.
